Project Overview

The Challenge

City residents needed a more efficient way to report urban issues like potholes, broken streetlights, and garbage accumulation. The existing reporting system was cumbersome and lacked transparency, leading to low citizen engagement.

The challenge was to design an intuitive mobile application that would simplify the reporting process, increase citizen participation, and provide transparency in issue resolution.

The Solution

Some friends and I designed Suara Kota, a user-friendly mobile app that allows citizens to easily report city issues with just a few taps. The app includes features like photo uploading, GPS location tagging, and real-time status tracking.

The design focuses on simplicity, accessibility, and transparency, ensuring that users of all ages and technical backgrounds can effectively use the application to contribute to city improvement.

Design Process

Research

Conducted user interviews and analyzed existing reporting systems to understand pain points and opportunities.

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity wireframes to establish the app structure and user flow before visual design.

UI Design

Developed a clean, accessible interface with a focus on usability and visual hierarchy.

Prototyping

Built interactive prototypes to test the user experience and gather feedback for iterations.

Key Features

Photo Reporting

Users can easily take photos of issues and attach them to their reports for clearer communication.

GPS Location

Automatic GPS tagging ensures accurate location data for faster issue resolution.

Status Tracking

Real-time updates on report status keep users informed about the progress of their submissions.

Category Selection

Intuitive category system helps users classify their reports for appropriate department routing.

Notifications

Push notifications alert users when their reports are received, assigned, and resolved.

Gamification

Points and badges system encourages continued citizen participation and engagement.
